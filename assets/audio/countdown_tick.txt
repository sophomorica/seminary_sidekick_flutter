# countdown_tick.txt → will become countdown_tick.wav

## Sound description
A soft, woody "tock" — like a muted woodblock or a clock's second hand heard
from across the room. Plays once per second during the final 5 seconds of a
group-quiz question, so it must be pleasant on repeat and create urgency
without anxiety. Dry (little/no reverb), rounded attack, no metallic ring.

## Where to source it
- freesound.org — search "woodblock tick", "soft tick percussion", "clock tock single"
- Pixabay audio — "countdown tick", "woodblock"
- Zapsplat — Sound Effects → Clocks & Timers → single tick samples

## Expected duration and format
~80–150ms, 44.1kHz .wav, mono is fine. Normalize a touch below the
correct.wav level so five ticks in a row don't dominate the mix.

## References
The Kahoot in-round timer tick (but warmer/softer), or the Headspace
session-end bell's attack with the tail cut off.
